Death Spiral Definition (UNC Kenan-Flagler): A death spiral occurs when declining volume forces fixed costs to be spread over fewer units, raising per-unit cost, which in turn drives further volume reduction in a self-reinforcing cycle. For Peloton, this is demand-driven: post-pandemic subscriber attrition reduces the base over which fixed content, studio, and instructor costs are allocated, raising the effective cost per subscriber and potentially pressuring pricing or quality decisions that accelerate further churn.
